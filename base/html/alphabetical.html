ABOUT

Subjective Dictionary investigates how the construction of dictionaries shapes language and, in turn, our perception of reality. While conventional dictionaries aim to fix meaning, this project understands every word as a point that overlaps with many others, consisting of subjective interpretations shaped by culture, language, and personal experience. The website uses data from Urban Dictionary and positions entries according to keyword relatedness in their definitions, clustering them based on shared terms. Each entry is displayed as a circle functioning as a node, where size represents popularity and color indicates the time of submission. Through this method of mapping, these perspectives form a growing archive of diverse realities and a research tool for exploring how meaning shifts. Another mode presents entries in alphabetical order, reflecting a more traditional dictionary structure. By visualizing how interpretations change across different mapping techniques, the project reveals the richness and plurality within the words we think we share.
